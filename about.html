The Grei Show

The Grei Show is the creative identity of Gamel Reid — a Jamaican recording artist, producer, songwriter and multidisciplinary creator building a body of work that stretches beyond songs into visual art, literature, photography, technology and interactive experiences.

The story

Rooted in Jamaica. Built without borders.

Raised in Bull Bay and based in Kingston, Gamel Reid developed The Grei Show as more than a stage name. It became a framework for bringing together the many sides of his creative life: the performer, the producer, the writer, the engineer, the visual thinker and the entrepreneur.

His musical language draws from reggae, dancehall and Jamaican sound-system culture while remaining open to hip-hop, electronic music, cinematic composition and experimental textures. That range is not treated as a collection of separate genres. It is a single evolving vocabulary shaped by rhythm, atmosphere, storytelling and the instinct to make familiar forms feel new.

As a producer, he has contributed to records connected to Protoje, Blvk H3ro, Bay-C, Swnkah, Sugar Minott and other Jamaican artists. His work on Protoje's “Weed & Ting,” co-produced with Supa Dups for the album In Search of Lost Time, placed his production within a major international reggae release. His own catalogue continues the same restless approach, moving from introspective songwriting and psychedelic textures to direct dancehall energy and bass-heavy experimentation.

Independence is central to the project. Through Wheel It! Records, The Grei Show develops releases, collaborations and multimedia ideas on his own terms, treating each project as part of a larger creative ecosystem rather than an isolated product.

Selected journey

A catalogue built through collaboration and experimentation.

2016–2017

Bussweh Party and early production work

Established a distinctive role as producer, singer and rapper within Kingston's Bussweh collective, producing its debut project and helping shape records connected to Blvk H3ro.

2019

Production on “Blvk Peppa”

The Grei Show's production appeared on Blvk H3ro's album The Immortal Steppa, extending a creative relationship formed within Kingston's independent scene.

2020

Protoje — “Weed & Ting”

Co-produced with Supa Dups for Protoje's In Search of Lost Time, with The Grei Show also documented on keyboards and composition.

2022–2024

“Puff Puff Pass” expands

The original release grew into a collaboration with Bay-C of T.O.K, supported by an official video and coverage from international reggae publications.

2025–Now

A wider creative universe

New music, independent releases, photography, fiction, live experiences, games and digital projects continue to expand The Grei Show beyond the traditional boundaries of an artist website or record catalogue.
